Frequently Asked Questions

What should I look for when buying a used car?

When buying a used car, look for consistent body panel gaps and matching paint for signs of accidents, check service records for maintenance history, and get a pre-purchase inspection by a trusted mechanic.

Is it better to buy or lease a car?

Leasing a car requires less money upfront and has lower payments, but there are typically mileage restrictions and additional costs.

What's the best way to maintain my car resale value?

To maintain your car's resale value, prioritize regular, documented maintenance using genuine parts, keep the vehicle spotless inside and out, address minor issues promptly, and avoid aftermarket modifications

How often should I change my car's oil?

You should change your car's oil according to the recommendations in your owner's manual, which typically falls between 5,000 to 7,500 miles for modern vehicles, though this can vary significantly based on oil type, vehicle age, and driving conditions

When should I replace my car's tires?

Replace your car's tires when their tread depth falls below 2/32 of an inch (check with the Penny Test), when they are 6 years or older (or 10 years maximum, regardless of tread), or if you see any visible damage like cracks, bulges, or deep gashes
